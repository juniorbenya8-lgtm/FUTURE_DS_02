# FUTURE_DS_02
Customer Retention &amp; Churn Analysis using cohort analysis, lifetime metrics, and business insights to reduce customer loss
### Objective
This project analyzes customer subscription data to identify churn patterns, retention drivers, customer lifetime trends, and practical actions a subscription-based business can take to reduce customer loss.

### Business Questions
- Why are customers leaving the platform?
- Which customer segments are most likely to churn?
- How long do customers typically stay active?
- What actions can improve customer retention?

### Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel: dashboard, charts, summary tables and report
- Python: data simulation, cleaning and churn/cohort calculations

### Dataset
A realistic simulated SaaS subscription dataset was used. It includes customer signup dates, plans, region, acquisition channel, onboarding status, usage score, support tickets, churn status, churn reason, customer lifetime and estimated revenue.

### Key KPIs
- Total Customers: 855
- Active Customers: 680
- Churned Customers: 175
- Overall Churn Rate: 20.5%
- Average Customer Lifetime: 6.7 months
- Active Monthly Recurring Revenue: $38,903

### Key Insights
1. The overall churn rate is 20.5%, showing that retention is a major business priority.
2. Basic plan customers show the highest churn risk, while Premium and Enterprise customers generally stay longer.
3. The strongest churn reasons are price concerns, low usage and missing features.
4. Customers who complete onboarding have stronger retention than customers who only partially complete onboarding or never start.
5. Acquisition channel performance differs, meaning the business should not only focus on customer volume but also customer quality.

### Recommendations
- Strengthen onboarding during the first 30 days to help customers reach value faster.
- Monitor customers with low usage scores and trigger proactive retention campaigns.
- Review Basic plan pricing and introduce annual plans, bundles or targeted retention offers.
- Use churn reason feedback to prioritize missing features and product improvements.
- Track churn monthly using the dashboard to identify retention issues early.
